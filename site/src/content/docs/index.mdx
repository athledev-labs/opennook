OpenNook gives you the hard part for free: a polished window that lives in the
menu-bar notch, expands and collapses on hover, paints a proper frosted
backdrop, and ships with a settings shell and a global hotkey. Drop your own
SwiftUI view into the expanded surface and you have a notch app.

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Notch + floating window" icon="laptop">
    A notch-shaped panel that hover-expands into a floating surface, with a
    translucent backdrop and shimmer feedback overlay. You drive it through
    `NookKit` — you don't fork the chrome.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Modular components" icon="puzzle">
    Opt-in add-ons in `NookComponents`: a drop-files shelf, a priority

    surface. Each ships its own configuration, services, and optional global
    shortcut.

## Install

Add OpenNook to your `Package.swift`:

```swift
.package(url: "https://github.com/glendonC/opennook", from: "0.1.0")
```

Then depend on the products you need from your target:

```swift
.target(
  name: "MyApp",
  dependencies: [
    .product(name: "NookApp", package: "opennook"),
    .product(name: "NookComponents", package: "opennook"), // optional
  ]
)
```
